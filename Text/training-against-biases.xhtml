Training to overcome irrational biases

One key goal in Algorithmic Rationalism is training ourselves to overcome our irrational biases. This is because no matter how much we learn to understand the process of Rationality in theory, and no matter how many exercises we do to hone this faculty, our irrational biases will constantly threaten to interfere with the process, and are liable to interfere without us even consciously realizing that this has happened.

It has been suggested that part of the means by which to do this on matters of social justice is to put yourself into the other person's sues - a practice of Cognitive Empathy. However, there is still the danger of our irrational biases tricking us into thinking that we have done this when in fact we haven't.

Overcoming your biases requires a lot of skills that are best honed both with exercises done on one's own and done in a community of Algorithmic Rationalists. We have to learn to check our biases - and that means, recognizing when they crop up, recognizing how they warp our thoughts, and administering the appropriate remedies to our thoughts.

All this requires that we know about the main viases that exist - and that each of us knows to which biases we are especially seceptible. We must know the warning signs of these biases - and we must develop algorithms for helping us better discern when one of these irrational biases is affecting our thoughts.

Very importantly, though, this process involves training in the art of true open-mindedness, which means, healthy skepticism of your own beliefs, whatever they are, and a willingness to modify them should evidence and reason dictate that it is appropriate to do so.
