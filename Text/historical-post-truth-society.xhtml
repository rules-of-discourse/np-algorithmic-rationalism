The Post-Truth Society

We are living in what is often referred to as a "post-truth society" - a society in which regard for whether or not an assertion conformed to objective reality is at dangerously low levels. As a matter of fact, regard for this is so low, that many people go so far as to dismiss its very existence - believing that the truth is whatever you can convince enough people of, or whatever you hold as a heartfelt belief. Not uncommon, as a matter of fact, is the belief that consciousness is a precursor of the material world rather than the other way around - a view that not only lacks regard for what exists in objective reality, but which outright denies its existence.

Of course - this view is scientifically unfathomable, but in a world that many people believe to be less than fifteen-thousand years old (typically just above six-thousand years old) despite overwhelming scientific evidence that it is actually a few billion years old - few people are going to let scientific unfathomability get in the way of what they wish to hold as doctrine.

Efforts to champion the existence of objective reality are often met with derision, with accusations of elitism. These accusations of elitism are often based partially on the perception that the one championing its existence believes zirself to have a complete knowledge of what is in that objective reality - or at least an understanding that is partial, yet authoritative. This perception is, of course, a straw-figure assertion - as championing that objective reality exists does not constitute asserting one's self as an infallible authority of its contents.

This wonton disregard for objective reality has resulted in the shutdown of public discourse that has for a long time been regarded as essential to the democratic process. Granted, the process has always been flawed, having not only fallen short of perfection, but also fallen significantly short of what realistically ought to be the best possible for. But even those shortcomings were as a result of it having being hindered by the precursor of the very phenomena that in these days are shutting it down entirely - that being a disregard for objective reality that, though not always as extreme as it is today, has infected human history as far back as can be verified.

Part of this reason for the shutdown in public discourse is that, often, the two sides can no longer agree on any common basis for discussion. Without the acknowledgement of a common, objective reality, it is impossible for people of differing opinions to investigate what actually exists in that reality. As a result, the resolution of "agree to disagree", a resolution which in a sane society would be reserved only for disagreements whose resolution defies the current ability to resolve by investigation, is instead often lauded as the pinnacle of maturity for all disagreements, no matter how realistic it is to verify which of the statements, if any, is the correct one.

Therefore, instead of submitting to the authority of objective reality when an answer to the disagreement can be discovered, and "agreeing to disagree" when no answer can be discovered - the modus operandi has become to "agree to disagree" on matters for which stakes and emotional vesture are low, and sheer hostility when one or both sides has significant stakes or emotional vesture in their position.

When high stakes or emotional vesture cause someone to be so inflexible on their ideas as to cause this hostility, this decision is often justified either by fallacious reasoning that could be spotted a mile away by any well-trained Rationalist, or by an all-out dismissal of the importance of reason. This is done even if the opinion in question just so happens to be a correct one that could just as well have been backed by sound reason and evidence - because so low has the regard for objective reality sunk that such correct views are more frequently stumbled upon by sheer dumb luck than arrived at by the sound methodology of reason and evidence.
