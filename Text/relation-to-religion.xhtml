Relation to religion

As with most schools of Rationalism, the reigning theory on the God question is atheism. The existence of a Creator God is not seen as altogether impossible but as highly improbable - and of an Interventionist God even more imporbable.

That said - this reigning theory is not imposed as a requirement in Algorithmic Rationalism. On the other hand, this theory is not to be casually disregarded on the basis of some alternative being more emotionally satisfying, socially convenient, or politically convenient to believe. The actual position on how Algorithmic Rationalists ought to answer the God question is more nuanced than either of those two extremes.

There is only one sacrosanct in Algorithmic Rationalism - the commitment to follow the dictates of logic, reason, and evidence wherever they lead. This one sacrosanct does not contain any statement on theology. Not only does it not explicitly contain any statement on theology, but unlike the requirement to adhere to the methodologies of Algorithmic Rationalism, it isn't even implicitly in there.

That said - while this position of atheism is by no means an extension of the One Sacrosanct of Algorithmic Rationalism, we do believe it is the inevitable consequence of applying it to the God question. While terribly weak arguments for such a position (or even a more extreme position) do in fact exist, there are also some arguments for this position that are quite solid, are firmly backed by the weight of the evidence on the matter, and have sustained repeated checks. It may be possible for someone to apply the One Sacrosanct to the God question and a different answer - but it is extremely unlikely if the dictates of logic, reason, and evidence are truly followed in full.

That said - we wish to avoid a situation in which people blindly accept such an atheistic position simply because the community says that it is so. Also - even if we had incontrovertible proof that any perfect rationalist would embrace this atheistic position, we do not know anybody who is actually a perfect rationalist. Even the best of us are merely imperfect rationalists working on ourselves toward the goal of perfection. We would rather accept among us someone who genuinely attempts to follow the One Sacrosanct but who due to such imperfection lands on theism than someone who blindly accepts atheism simply because that's what the community says. Someone who lands on theism due to such an imperfection may have their theistic views challenged in the community in the form of people pointing out issues that they might have failed to consider - but they will be respected.

There is, however, one more reason why atheism is not a requirement for being regarded as an Algorithmic Rationalist. This is because there may be some people who have truly adopted Algorithmic Rationalism and are applying it to many areas of their life - but who for some reason haven't gotten around to, or simply aren't ready to apply it to the God question. They, too, are preferable to those who don't practice Rationalism at all but accept atheism for some reason other than logic, reason, or the weight of evidence.
